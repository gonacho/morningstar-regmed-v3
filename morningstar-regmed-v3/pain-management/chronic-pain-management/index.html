What Is Chronic Pain?

Chronic pain is any pain that lasts longer than three months. By this point, the pain has persisted well beyond the time most injuries and conditions need to heal, which means the pain itself has become the primary medical concern.

While there is no single cure for chronic pain, it is highly manageable through a combination of medical treatments and lifestyle changes. Modern pain management has advanced significantly, offering patients more effective options than ever before to reduce pain levels and restore daily function.

At Morning Star, we understand that chronic pain is deeply personal. It affects every aspect of your life, from work and sleep to relationships and mental health. That is why we take an individualized approach to every patient who walks through our doors.

Common Causes of Chronic Pain

Previous Injuries

Injuries from accidents, surgeries, or physical trauma can lead to chronic pain even after the original injury has healed, due to lasting nerve or tissue damage.

Nerve Damage

Neuropathy and other nerve conditions cause ongoing pain signals even when no active injury is present, creating persistent discomfort that interferes with daily activities.

Arthritis & Joint Conditions

Osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, and other joint conditions cause progressive inflammation and pain that worsens without proper management.

Spinal Conditions

Degenerative disc disease, spinal stenosis, and herniated discs are common sources of chronic back pain and neck pain.

Signs You May Be Dealing With Chronic Pain

Chronic pain presents differently for every person. The following are some of the most common indicators that your pain may require professional management:

Pain that persists for more than three months

Pain that returns repeatedly after treatment

Fatigue and difficulty sleeping due to pain

Reduced mobility and limited daily activities

Mood changes, including anxiety or depression

Increased sensitivity to pain or touch

When to Seek Professional Help

If your pain has lasted longer than three months, it has persisted beyond the time most injuries need to heal. This is a clear signal that professional pain management is needed. You should also seek help if:

  • • Over-the-counter medications no longer provide relief
  • • Pain is interfering with your work, relationships, or sleep
  • • You are relying on pain medication more frequently
  • • Your pain is getting progressively worse
  • • You feel hopeless or depressed because of your pain

Frequently Asked Questions

What is chronic pain?
Chronic pain is defined as any pain that persists for longer than three months. Unlike acute pain, which serves as a warning signal for injury, chronic pain continues well beyond the normal healing period. It can stem from an initial injury, an ongoing condition, or sometimes have no identifiable cause.
Can chronic pain be cured?
Chronic pain is typically managed rather than cured. While some underlying causes can be treated and resolved, many chronic pain conditions require ongoing management strategies. The good news is that with the right combination of medical treatments, lifestyle modifications, and professional support, most patients achieve significant pain reduction and improved quality of life.
How do I know if my pain is chronic?
If your pain has lasted longer than three months, it has persisted beyond the time most injuries need to heal and is generally considered chronic. Other indicators include pain that keeps returning even after treatment, pain that gradually worsens over time, or pain that significantly interferes with your daily activities, sleep, or emotional well-being.
What treatment options are available for chronic pain?
Morning Star offers multiple approaches to chronic pain management, including regenerative therapy, neuromodulation techniques, platelet-rich plasma therapy, and targeted pain management interventions. We develop personalized treatment plans that combine the most effective therapies for your specific condition.
What lifestyle changes help with chronic pain?
Regular low-impact exercise, maintaining a healthy weight, practicing stress management techniques, getting adequate sleep, and following an anti-inflammatory diet can all contribute to better pain management. Our team provides personalized lifestyle guidance as part of your treatment plan.
