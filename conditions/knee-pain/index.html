Understanding Knee Pain

Knee pain is one of the most common musculoskeletal complaints in the United States, with peak prevalence occurring between ages 55 and 64. The knee joint bears the full weight of the body during standing, walking, running, and climbing, making it especially vulnerable to injury and wear.

Meniscus tears are among the most frequent knee injuries. The meniscus is a C-shaped piece of cartilage that cushions the space between your shinbone and thighbone. When it tears, you may experience a popping sensation followed by swelling, stiffness, and pain, especially when twisting or rotating the knee. Left untreated, a torn meniscus can accelerate joint deterioration and lead to chronic knee problems.

Orthotics such as knee braces provide support and stability, though they are most effective as part of a comprehensive treatment plan. Pain that persists beyond one week typically indicates a larger issue requiring professional evaluation.

Common Causes of Knee Pain

Meniscus Tears

Tears in the cartilage cushion of the knee, often caused by sudden twisting or rotating movements. Symptoms include popping sensations, swelling, and pain during movement.

Osteoarthritis

Progressive cartilage deterioration in the knee joint causes bones to grind against each other, resulting in pain, stiffness, and reduced mobility. This is the most common form of arthritis affecting the knee.

Ligament Injuries

ACL, MCL, and other ligament tears or sprains commonly occur during sports or sudden directional changes, causing instability and significant pain.

Overuse and Tendinitis

Repetitive stress from running, jumping, or climbing stairs can inflame the tendons around the knee, leading to chronic discomfort and reduced function.

Symptoms to Watch For

Knee pain presents differently depending on the underlying cause. Pay close attention to these warning signs:

  • • Popping or clicking sounds during movement
  • • Swelling and stiffness in or around the knee
  • • Pain when twisting, rotating, or bending the knee
  • • Difficulty straightening the knee fully
  • • The knee locking or giving way unexpectedly
  • • Pain that worsens with stairs, squatting, or kneeling
  • • Warmth or redness around the joint

Treatment Options at Morning Star

Our non-surgical approach to knee pain treatment focuses on reducing inflammation, promoting natural healing, and restoring function. Your personalized plan may include one or more of the following therapies.

Platelet-Rich Plasma (PRP) Therapy

PRP therapy uses growth factors concentrated from your own blood to accelerate tissue repair and reduce inflammation in the knee joint. This treatment is especially effective for soft tissue injuries and early-stage arthritis.

Hyaluronic acid injections restore the natural lubrication in your knee joint, reducing bone-on-bone friction and providing significant pain relief for patients with osteoarthritis.

Regenerative Therapy

Our regenerative therapy program supports the body's natural healing processes, helping to repair damaged cartilage and joint tissue without surgery. Many patients see meaningful improvement within weeks of starting treatment.

When to Seek Professional Help

Knee pain that lasts beyond one week usually signals a condition that needs professional attention. You should schedule an evaluation at our St. Peters clinic if:

  • • Pain persists for more than one week despite rest
  • • You cannot bear weight on the affected knee
  • • The knee is visibly swollen, red, or warm to the touch

  • • The knee feels unstable or locks during movement

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most common causes of knee pain?
The most common causes of knee pain include osteoarthritis, meniscus tears, ligament injuries (such as ACL and MCL tears), tendinitis, bursitis, and overuse from repetitive activities. Excess body weight also places additional stress on the knee joints, accelerating cartilage breakdown and joint deterioration over time.
When should I see a doctor for knee pain?
You should see a doctor if your knee pain lasts beyond one week, as this usually indicates a larger underlying issue. Seek immediate care if you heard a popping sound at the time of injury, cannot bear weight on the knee, experience significant swelling, or notice the knee locking or giving way during movement.
What treatment options are available for knee pain?
Morning Star offers multiple non-surgical treatment options for knee pain, including hyaluronic acid injections to restore joint lubrication, platelet-rich plasma (PRP) therapy to promote tissue repair, regenerative therapy to support natural healing, and comprehensive pain management programs tailored to each patient.
Can knee pain be prevented?
Many knee problems can be prevented or delayed by maintaining a healthy weight, strengthening the muscles that support the knee (especially the quadriceps and hamstrings), using proper form during exercise, wearing supportive footwear, and warming up before physical activity. Low-impact exercises like swimming and cycling are excellent for long-term knee health.
What age group is most affected by knee pain?
Knee pain reaches its peak prevalence between ages 55 and 64, primarily due to osteoarthritis and accumulated wear on the joint. However, knee pain can occur at any age. Younger adults frequently experience knee injuries from sports and high-impact activities, while middle-aged adults may develop pain from overuse or early-stage arthritis.
