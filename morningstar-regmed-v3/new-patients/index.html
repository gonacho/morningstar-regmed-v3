New Patient Information

Walking into a new clinic can feel uncertain, especially when you have been dealing with pain for a long time. We want to make your first visit as straightforward as possible. Here is exactly what to expect when you come to Morning Star in St. Peters.

Schedule Your First Visit

What to Expect at Your First Visit

Your initial appointment at Morning Star is a consultation, not a sales pitch. We spend 45 to 60 minutes with every new patient to understand your situation before recommending anything.

1. Health History Review

We review your complete medical history, current medications, previous treatments, and diagnostic imaging. Bring any relevant medical records, imaging results, or medication lists.

2. Physical Evaluation

Our provider performs a hands-on examination focused on your pain areas, nerve function, joint mobility, and range of motion. Additional diagnostic testing may be ordered based on findings.

3. Treatment Discussion

We explain our diagnosis, discuss all available treatment options, and work with you to build a personalized care plan. You will leave with a clear understanding of next steps.

How to Prepare for Your Appointment

  • ✓ Bring your insurance card and a valid photo ID
  • ✓ Bring a list of current medications and supplements
  • ✓ Bring any recent imaging (X-rays, MRIs) or lab results related to your condition
  • ✓ Write down your main questions or concerns so you do not forget them
  • ✓ Arrive 15 minutes early to complete any remaining paperwork
  • ✓ Wear comfortable clothing that allows easy access to the affected area

Insurance and Payment

Morning Star accepts major medical insurance plans. We recommend contacting your insurance provider before your first visit to confirm coverage for the services discussed. Our front desk team can also help verify your benefits.

For patients who prefer financing, we accept CareCredit. Visit our insurance and payment page for more details on accepted plans and payment options.
