Understanding Back Pain

Back pain affects roughly 80 percent of adults at some point in their lives. While minor cases often resolve on their own with rest and self-care, chronic or severe back pain requires professional attention to prevent long-term damage and restore normal function.

Poor posture and injuries remain the primary drivers. In today's digital world, hunching over phones and computers for hours each day has dramatically increased the number of people experiencing back problems, even among younger adults. When you factor in the physical toll of repetitive motions, heavy lifting, or sedentary lifestyles, the scope of the problem becomes clear.

Treatment ranges from simple rest combined with heat and cold therapy for minor cases to comprehensive pain management programs for chronic conditions. The key is working with medical providers who can determine whether your pain is muscular, spinal, or nerve-related, because the source of your pain dictates the most effective treatment approach.

Common Causes of Back Pain

Disc Issues

Herniated discs, bulging discs, and degenerative disc disease can press on spinal nerves, causing localized pain or radiating symptoms that travel through the back and down into the legs.

Muscle Strain

Lifting heavy objects incorrectly, sudden awkward movements, sports injuries, and auto accidents can strain muscles or damage the ligaments and tendons that support the spine.

Spinal Stenosis

A narrowing of the spinal canal that puts pressure on the spinal cord and nerves. This condition is most common in adults over 50 and causes pain, numbness, and weakness in the back and legs.

Degenerative Conditions

Osteoarthritis and other age-related changes in the spine gradually break down cartilage, discs, and joint surfaces, leading to chronic stiffness and pain.

Postural Habits

Slouching, hunching over screens, and poor workplace ergonomics place excessive stress on the spine, muscles, and ligaments. Over months and years, these habits create structural imbalances that produce chronic pain, even in younger adults.

Symptoms to Watch For

Back pain symptoms vary widely depending on the cause and location of the problem. Common signs that warrant attention include:

  • • Dull, aching pain in the lower, middle, or upper back
  • • Sharp or shooting pain that radiates down the legs
  • • Stiffness and reduced range of motion
  • • Muscle spasms or persistent tightness
  • • Pain that worsens with sitting, standing, or bending
  • • Numbness or tingling in the legs or feet
  • • Pain that disrupts sleep or makes it difficult to find a comfortable position

Treatment Options at Morning Star

At Morning Star in St. Peters, we take a comprehensive approach to back pain treatment. Our team begins with a thorough evaluation to identify the exact source of your pain, then develops a personalized plan that may include one or more of the following therapies.

Regenerative Therapy

Our regenerative therapy program uses advanced treatments that promote natural healing of damaged tissues and reduce inflammation in the spine and surrounding structures. This approach helps the body repair disc, muscle, and ligament damage without surgery.

Chronic Pain Management

Our chronic pain management program provides targeted interventions to reduce pain levels and improve function. For patients dealing with long-standing back problems, this structured approach addresses both the physical and functional aspects of chronic pain.

Platelet-Rich Plasma (PRP) Therapy

PRP therapy concentrates growth factors from your own blood and delivers them directly to the injured area, accelerating tissue repair and reducing inflammation. This treatment is effective for disc injuries, muscle tears, and ligament damage.

When to Seek Professional Help

While minor back pain often improves with rest, you should seek professional care when pain is recurring, intensifying, or beginning to impact your quality of life. Specific warning signs include:

  • • Pain lasting more than two weeks without improvement
  • • Numbness, tingling, or weakness in your legs
  • • Pain following a traumatic injury or accident
  • • Difficulty sleeping due to back pain
  • • Pain that prevents you from working or performing daily tasks
  • • Loss of bladder or bowel control (seek immediate care)

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most common causes of back pain?
The most common causes of back pain include poor posture, muscle strains from lifting heavy objects, herniated or bulging discs, and injuries from accidents or falls. Sedentary lifestyles and spending long hours hunching over phones or computers have also become leading contributors to back pain in recent years.
When is back pain serious enough to see a doctor?
You should see a doctor when back pain is recurring, lasts more than a few weeks, or begins to impact your quality of life. Seek immediate care if your pain is accompanied by numbness in your legs, difficulty walking, loss of bladder or bowel control, or if it follows a traumatic injury such as a fall or car accident.
Are there home remedies for back pain?
For minor back pain, rest combined with alternating heat and cold therapy can provide temporary relief. Over-the-counter anti-inflammatory medications, gentle stretching, and maintaining good posture also help. However, if your pain persists beyond a few days or worsens, professional evaluation is recommended to rule out structural or nerve-related causes.
Can back pain be caused by nerve problems?
Yes, back pain is frequently caused by nerve-related issues such as a pinched nerve, sciatica, or spinal stenosis. These conditions occur when spinal structures compress or irritate nearby nerves, producing pain that often radiates into the buttocks, legs, or feet. At Morning Star, our providers perform thorough evaluations to determine whether your pain is muscular, spinal, or nerve-related.
What treatment options does Morning Star offer for back pain?
Morning Star offers a range of non-invasive treatments for back pain, including regenerative therapy to promote tissue healing, chronic pain management programs, and platelet-rich plasma (PRP) therapy to accelerate recovery. Our team creates personalized treatment plans based on the source and severity of your pain.
