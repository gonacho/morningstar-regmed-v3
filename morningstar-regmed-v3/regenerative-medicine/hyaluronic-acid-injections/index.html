Hyaluronic Acid Injections in St. Peters, MO

Minimally invasive joint lubrication therapy designed to relieve pain, reduce stiffness, and restore mobility, without surgery or heavy medication.

Schedule a Consultation

What Are Hyaluronic Acid Injections?

Hyaluronic acid is a naturally occurring substance found in joint fluid that acts as a lubricant and shock absorber. When joints become damaged or arthritic, hyaluronic acid levels decrease, leading to increased friction, stiffness, and pain. Hyaluronic acid injections replenish this vital fluid, restoring cushioning and improving joint function.

At Morning Star Neuropathy & Pain Treatment Center, this minimally invasive treatment offers a faster recovery path compared to surgery, without the side effects of long-term medication use. It is especially effective for patients with mild to moderate joint conditions who want to maintain an active lifestyle.

Hyaluronic acid injections are part of our regenerative medicine program in St. Peters. We also offer platelet-rich plasma (PRP) therapy and exosome-based regenerative therapy for patients who need a different approach or combination of treatments.

How the Treatment Works

1. Joint Assessment

We evaluate the affected joint through physical examination and imaging to determine the degree of cartilage loss and confirm that hyaluronic acid injections are appropriate.

2. Precision Injection

Hyaluronic acid is injected directly into the joint space using guided techniques. A local anesthetic is applied to ensure comfort throughout the brief procedure.

3. Progressive Relief

As the hyaluronic acid restores joint lubrication, patients typically notice reduced pain and improved mobility over the following weeks. Follow-up visits track your progress.

Who Benefits from Hyaluronic Acid Injections?

This treatment is most effective for patients experiencing joint pain from cartilage wear or early-stage arthritis. Patients visit our St. Peters office for hyaluronic acid injections to address:

  • ✓ Knee pain from osteoarthritis or cartilage loss
  • ✓ Joint pain in hips, shoulders, and ankles
  • ✓ Stiffness that limits daily movement or activity
  • ✓ Mild to moderate osteoarthritis
  • ✓ Joint discomfort that has not improved with oral medication
  • ✓ Patients who want to delay or avoid joint replacement surgery

During your consultation, our team will review your medical history and imaging to confirm whether hyaluronic acid injections are the right choice for your specific condition.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do hyaluronic acid injections actually work?
Effectiveness depends on the severity of your joint condition. Most patients with mild to moderate osteoarthritis experience meaningful relief, including reduced pain, improved mobility, and better joint function. For advanced joint degeneration, results may be more limited. Our team will assess your condition and discuss realistic expectations during your consultation.
How long do the results last?
Results typically last between 4 to 6 months, though some patients experience relief for up to a year. The duration varies based on the severity of your condition, the joint treated, and your activity level. Repeat injections can be administered to maintain the benefits over time.
Who should not get hyaluronic acid injections?
Patients with bleeding disorders, certain allergies (particularly to hyaluronic acid or avian products, depending on the formulation), or an active infection in or around the joint should not receive these injections. Our clinical team reviews your full medical history before recommending treatment to ensure your safety.
What should I expect during the procedure?
The procedure is straightforward and performed in-office. After cleaning the injection site, a local anesthetic may be applied to minimize discomfort. The hyaluronic acid is then injected directly into the joint. The entire process typically takes 15 to 30 minutes, and most patients return to normal activities the same day.
What is the recovery time?
Recovery is minimal. You may experience mild swelling or stiffness at the injection site for 24 to 48 hours. We recommend avoiding strenuous activity for a day or two after treatment. Most patients resume their daily routine immediately and notice progressive improvement over the following weeks.
