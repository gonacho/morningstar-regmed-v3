What Is Medical Weight Loss?

Medical weight loss is a physician-supervised program that uses FDA-approved medications, lab-based monitoring, and structured lifestyle guidance to help patients lose weight safely. It is not a diet plan sold in a box. It is not a meal replacement shake. It is a clinical treatment backed by research and overseen by a medical team.

Most commercial diet programs ignore the hormonal and metabolic factors that make sustained weight loss so difficult. When your body fights back with increased hunger signals and a slowing metabolism, discipline alone cannot overcome biology. That is where medical intervention changes the equation.

At our St. Peters medical clinic, we pair proven medications with ongoing clinical oversight. Every patient receives an individualized plan, regular progress assessments, and dosing adjustments based on how their body responds. The goal is not just weight loss. The goal is weight loss that lasts.

How Semaglutide & Tirzepatide Work

Semaglutide and tirzepatide belong to a class of medications called GLP-1 receptor agonists. They mimic a natural hormone your gut produces after eating, sending signals to your brain that you are full. The result: reduced appetite, fewer cravings, and a more regulated relationship with food.

These medications also help regulate blood sugar levels, which is especially relevant for patients with insulin resistance or prediabetes. By stabilizing blood sugar, they reduce the energy crashes and intense hunger spikes that often derail conventional diets.

Tirzepatide works on two receptor pathways (GLP-1 and GIP), which clinical trials have shown may produce greater weight loss in some patients compared to single-receptor medications. Semaglutide targets the GLP-1 pathway alone and has its own strong track record in published research.

Both medications are administered as weekly injections. Our clinical team determines which medication fits your health profile, then adjusts dosing gradually to minimize side effects and maximize results.

The Process: From Evaluation to Results

1. Medical Evaluation

Your program begins with a thorough review of your medical history, current medications, lab work, and weight loss goals. We identify any underlying conditions that could affect treatment, then determine whether semaglutide or tirzepatide is the right fit.

2. Medication Selection & Dosing

Based on your evaluation, we prescribe the medication best suited to your body and health profile. Dosing starts low and increases over several weeks, allowing your system to adjust while we monitor for any side effects.

3. Ongoing Monitoring

Regular follow-up visits let us track your weight, vitals, and overall response to treatment. We adjust your medication dosage and plan as needed. You are never left to figure things out on your own.

4. Lifestyle Guidance

Medication works best alongside sustainable habits. Our team provides nutritional recommendations, activity guidance, and practical strategies for maintaining your progress long after the prescription ends.

Why Weight Loss Matters for Pain & Neuropathy Patients

If you live with chronic pain or peripheral neuropathy, carrying extra weight makes both conditions harder to manage. Every additional pound increases the mechanical load on your knees, hips, and lower back. Over time, that added stress accelerates joint degeneration and amplifies pain signals.

Excess weight also fuels systemic inflammation, a known driver of neuropathic symptoms. Fat tissue produces inflammatory markers that can worsen nerve damage and slow recovery from treatment. For patients already dealing with numbness, tingling, or burning in their feet, this creates a cycle that is difficult to break without medical support.

Losing even 10 to 15 percent of body weight has been shown to reduce inflammatory markers, improve nerve function, and decrease pain severity. For many of our patients in St. Peters, medical weight loss is not a cosmetic decision. It is a critical part of their overall treatment plan.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who qualifies for medical weight loss?
Adults with a BMI of 30 or higher are generally candidates for GLP-1 medications. Adults with a BMI of 27 or higher may also qualify if they have a weight-related condition such as type 2 diabetes, high blood pressure, or sleep apnea. During your initial evaluation, our clinical team reviews your full medical history and current health markers to determine whether semaglutide or tirzepatide is appropriate for you.
How long does treatment last?
Most patients follow a structured program for 3 to 6 months, though some continue longer depending on their goals and progress. Medication dosing increases gradually over the first several weeks to reduce side effects. Our team reassesses your plan at every visit and adjusts the timeline based on how your body responds.
What are the common side effects of semaglutide and tirzepatide?
The most frequently reported side effects include mild nausea, reduced appetite, and occasional digestive discomfort. These symptoms typically appear during the dose-escalation phase and improve within a few weeks as your body adjusts. Our clinical team monitors you closely and can modify your dosing schedule if side effects become bothersome.
Does insurance cover medical weight loss medications?
Coverage varies by plan and carrier. Some insurers cover GLP-1 medications for weight management, while others limit coverage to diabetes treatment. Our staff can help verify your benefits before you begin. For patients without coverage, we offer transparent pricing so you know exactly what to expect.
How quickly will I see results?
Many patients notice reduced appetite and early weight changes within the first 2 to 4 weeks. Typical weight loss ranges from 1 to 3 pounds per week once you reach your target dose. Clinical studies show that patients using these medications alongside dietary and activity changes achieve significantly more weight loss than with lifestyle changes alone. Our team sets realistic milestones based on your starting point and health profile.
Will I regain the weight after stopping medication?
Long-term success depends on the habits you build during treatment. Semaglutide and tirzepatide help retrain your appetite signals and relationship with food, giving you a foundation to maintain progress. Our program includes nutritional guidance and lifestyle coaching specifically designed to help you sustain results after the medication phase ends.
