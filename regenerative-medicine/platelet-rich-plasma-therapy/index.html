Platelet-Rich Plasma (PRP) Therapy in St. Peters, MO

Use your body's own concentrated platelets to accelerate healing, reduce pain, and repair damaged tissue, without surgery.

What Is PRP Therapy?

Platelet-rich plasma (PRP) therapy is a regenerative treatment that uses concentrated platelets from your own blood to promote healing. Platelets contain growth factors that play a critical role in tissue repair, making PRP a natural and effective option for chronic joint conditions, soft tissue injuries, and osteoarthritis.

At Morning Star Neuropathy & Pain Treatment Center, PRP sessions are performed in-office and typically take 45 minutes to an hour. The procedure involves a simple blood draw, processing in a centrifuge, and a targeted injection into the affected area. A numbing cream is applied beforehand to minimize any discomfort.

PRP therapy is one of several regenerative medicine treatments we offer at our St. Peters clinic. Depending on your condition, our team may also recommend exosome-based regenerative therapy or hyaluronic acid injections as part of your recovery plan.

How PRP Therapy Works

1. Blood Draw

A small sample of blood is drawn from your arm, similar to a routine blood test. The process is quick and straightforward.

2. Platelet Concentration

Your blood is placed in a centrifuge that separates the platelets and concentrates them into a small volume of plasma rich in growth factors.

3. Targeted Injection

The concentrated PRP is injected directly into the damaged tissue or joint, where the growth factors stimulate your body's natural repair process.

Conditions Treated with PRP

PRP therapy is effective for musculoskeletal conditions where the body's natural healing response needs a boost. Patients come to our St. Peters office for PRP treatment of:

  • ✓ Knee pain from osteoarthritis or sports injuries
  • ✓ Back pain related to disc or tissue damage
  • ✓ Joint pain in shoulders, hips, and wrists
  • ✓ Rotator cuff tears and tendon injuries
  • ✓ Ligament sprains and soft tissue damage
  • ✓ Chronic inflammation that has not responded to other treatments

Our clinical team reviews your imaging and medical history before recommending PRP. If a different approach would serve you better, we will let you know.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does PRP therapy work?
PRP therapy begins with a simple blood draw from your arm. The blood is then placed in a centrifuge to separate and concentrate the platelets. This platelet-rich plasma, packed with growth factors, is injected directly into the injured or damaged area, stimulating your body's natural healing response and accelerating tissue repair.
What conditions does PRP therapy treat?
PRP is effective for a range of musculoskeletal conditions including osteoarthritis, tendon injuries, ligament sprains, rotator cuff tears, and soft tissue damage. It is also used to support healing after certain procedures. Our team will evaluate your condition to determine if PRP is the most appropriate option.
How long does a PRP session take?
A typical PRP session takes approximately 45 minutes to one hour from start to finish. This includes the blood draw, processing time in the centrifuge, and the injection itself. Most patients are able to return to their normal routine the same day.
Is PRP therapy painful?
Most patients experience minimal discomfort during PRP therapy. A topical numbing cream is applied to the treatment area before the injection to reduce any pain. Some patients report mild soreness at the injection site for a day or two afterward, which typically resolves on its own.
How many PRP sessions will I need?
The number of sessions varies depending on the severity of your condition and your body's response to treatment. Many patients see significant improvement after 1 to 3 sessions spaced several weeks apart. Our team will create a personalized treatment plan based on your specific needs and progress.
